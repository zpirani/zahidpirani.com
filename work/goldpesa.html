Connected surfaces

GoldPesa is a brand owned and operated by Trilogy Precious Metals DMCC, a gold trading company holding a precious metals licence in the UAE since 2013. With offices in Dubai and London, the team brought together engineers, quantitative researchers, finance specialists, and design to build a gold-backed, tokenised structured product — combining the stability of physical gold with the liquidity and growth dynamics of a DeFi token.

I was the sole designer on the work, responsible for the brand identity and every production interface for token holders: the token presale (launched October 16, 2021), the decentralised exchange, the cross-chain bridge, staking, and the broader Platform.

The problem

GoldPesa's tokenomics were the strategic heart of the product. The GPX token is minted at the spot price of gold plus a 1% fee. Half of that 1% feeds a proprietary trading strategy called the PAWN, whose profits buy back and burn GPX from the market — creating demand and reducing supply simultaneously. Increased volatility feeds more fees into the PAWN, which compounds the effect.

That mechanism made the token compelling. It also created five hard design problems:

  1. Explain a complex financial product to a crypto-native audience that skims. Gold-backed structured products are rare in DeFi; the value proposition had to land in seconds.
  2. Operate across two blockchains. Minting on Ethereum; gas-cheap actions on Polygon. Users needed a frictionless way to bridge between them.
  3. Bring self-custody into the workflow. MetaMask and Trust Wallet were the entry points, not a centralised exchange account — and most gold investors had never touched either.
  4. Surface real-time PAWN performance transparently. The buyback-and-burn data was the trust-building proof point, not a footnote.
  5. One coherent brand and experience across five surfaces: presale, DEX, bridge, staking, and customer-facing collateral.

Users

The audience clustered into three groups:

  • Crypto-native investors comfortable with MetaMask, Uniswap, and gas fees, looking for an asset class that wasn't another speculative coin.
  • Gold investors discovering crypto — more conservative, attracted by the gold backing but unfamiliar with wallet-based UX.
  • DeFi power users interested in the tokenomics mechanics themselves — yield, buybacks, burn schedules.

The system

Five connected surfaces, one design language.

1. GoldPesa Platform

The hub. Account, portfolio, token information, and entry points to every other surface.

2. Token presale UI — launched October 16, 2021

The first public surface. Users connected MetaMask or Trust Wallet and swapped ETH for the GoldPesa Option token (GPO). This is where brand met functional UI for the first time.

3. Uniswap-powered DEX

The interface for token holders to deposit, bridge, stake, and swap their tokens on the GoldPesa Decentralized Exchange.

4. Polygon Bridge

The cross-chain workflow moving holdings from Ethereum to Polygon and back — abstracting the complexity into a single confirmation step.

5. Staking Contract UI

The interface for token holders to lock GPX in exchange for rewards generated by the PAWN trading strategy.

6. Brand system & presentation

Wordmark, colour, type, iconography, tradeshow banners and booth design, investor letters, pitch deck.

Reflection

GoldPesa was where I became a systems thinker before I had the vocabulary for it. As the sole designer, I held the whole product in my head at once — brand, presale, DEX, bridge, staking, investor letters. Every decision in one surface had to propagate to the others. It taught me what designing without a system feels like, and why building one is non-negotiable at scale. Every design system project I've worked on since traces back to lessons from this one.

The two audiences were the hard part. Crypto-native users skim and don't read disclaimers. Gold investors read everything and distrust the technology. The product had to convince both at the same moment — there was no opportunity to A/B-test the brand voice or simplify the workflow for one cohort. I learned to design for the more sceptical of the two as the default: if the wording, the iconography, and the flow held up for the gold investor, the crypto-native user could handle it. The reverse was never true.

If I were starting again today, I'd build the brand and design system before the product surfaces, not in parallel with them. The five connected products would have shipped faster and more consistently if the system had been the first deliverable rather than a concurrent one. The same lesson played out at Axi a few years later when ATP started without a mature design system and product velocity only caught up once the system did. The systems thinking I now apply at Shell across Terminal Cockpit and TPC started here.
