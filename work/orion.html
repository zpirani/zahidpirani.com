ORION — Inventory Intelligence Dashboard

A real-time terminal inventory platform for Shell's trading and scheduling teams. ORION replaced fragmented, manually assembled Supply updates with a live Databricks-fed view of customer positions, movements, and constraints — refreshed every 15 minutes.

The Business Problem

Traders and schedulers at Shell depended on the Supply team for inventory and constraint updates. Every time a trader needed to know whether a customer had enough LNG to fulfil an upcoming deal, they had to contact Supply, wait for a response, and work from information that was already out of date by the time it arrived.

This created a cascade of problems: slower decision-making, increased operational risk, and key insights — movements, constraints, available-to-sell positions — scattered across systems and communications channels with no single source of truth.

Strategic Objective

Provide a single source of truth that accelerates decisions, reduces risk, and improves utilisation by surfacing current and forecast inventory, movements, constraints, and available-to-sell (ATS) positions in one place — in near real-time.

The Solution

ORION aggregates operational terminal data from Databricks and presents it as a set of intuitive dashboard views. A trader opening ORION can instantly see:

  • Current inventory volumes (in bbl) by customer, ranked and filterable by product
  • Total terminal capacity vs. utilisation, with free capacity and ullage indicators
  • Receipts, withdrawals, and pipeline transfers — with ETA/ETD and inventory impact
  • Customer-level storage balances and constraints (heel/min/max/maintenance)
  • 7 and 30-day inventory forecast with projected breach alerts
  • Available-to-sell (ATS) positions — updated every 15 minutes from Databricks

Core Dashboard Views

The MVP comprises five interconnected views, each answering a distinct question a trader or planner faces during their day:

  • Customer Inventory View — ranked horizontal bar chart by customer, filterable by product (LNG/LPG), with customer-level KPIs: contracted vs. actual, min, max, and alerts
  • Product Inventory View — donut chart for product splits, volume cards, and day-by-day stock movement
  • Forecast & Constraints View — trend line with projected breach alerts and nomination pipeline (incoming vs. outgoing)
  • Capacity & Utilisation View — total capacity, utilisation %, free capacity, and max daily drawdown with gauges and mini charts per tank
  • Movements View — vessel arrivals and quantities, truck/pipeline in/outflow, customer-level deltas, and estimated completion times

Technology

Databricks serves as the single source of truth for all inventory data, with a 15-minute refresh rate across all views. The MVP visibility model gives all users access to all customer volumes, with a more granular RBAC model planned for future phases.

Outcome

ORION gives Shell's trading and scheduling teams a fundamentally faster and safer way to work. By removing the dependency on manual Supply updates and replacing it with a live, accurate dashboard, the platform frees traders to focus on making decisions rather than chasing data. Success will be measured by fewer Supply requests, faster decision cycles, improved ATS accuracy, and fewer last-minute operational escalations.
