Context

Multiple teams at Axi were building across mobile, web, crypto, and account-management products. Without a unified system, each product team risked drifting into its own visual language, its own component patterns, its own definition of "primary button." That drift compounds: it slows delivery, fragments user experience across products, and creates design debt that becomes impossibly expensive to repay later.

The three problems the system had to solve

  1. Scalability. Multiple teams building in parallel across multiple products needed a single source of truth for visual and interaction patterns. Without it, every product team would solve the same problem differently — every time.
  2. Consistency. Trading experiences must feel seamless across surfaces. A buy button, a chart annotation, a confirmation modal — these should look and behave identically whether on mobile, web, or inside the Client Portal. Inconsistency erodes trust in a regulated context.
  3. Speed and efficiency. Without shared components, product teams duplicate work. Designers re-design buttons; developers re-implement them. The system had to accelerate delivery, not just constrain it.

The foundations

Atomic design (Brad Frost methodology)

The system is structured around Brad Frost's atomic design methodology — breaking the interface into atoms (basic styles), molecules (small reusable patterns), organisms (more complex composites), templates, and pages.

The decision to follow an established methodology rather than invent one was deliberate: it accelerated the system's foundation, gave designers and developers a shared vocabulary, and made onboarding new team members faster.

8-point grid system

Every dimension, padding, and margin in the system is a multiple of 8. This rule covers spacing, sizing, and alignment — and creates visual consistency that designers don't have to consciously enforce on every screen.

Accessible colour and typography (AA-compliant)

The colour palette was tuned against Material Design's accessibility research to maintain AA contrast ratios across all states (default, hover, disabled, focused, selected, error). Typography followed the same discipline — type pairings, sizes, and weights chosen for legibility across the full system rather than aesthetic preference.

The system in practice

Component library

The component library spans mobile and web — atoms, molecules, organisms — with usage guidelines and accessibility annotations for each. Components are tied to a Storybook implementation that engineering teams use directly.

Cross-product consistency

The Axi Design System serves ATP (mobile + web), ATP Crypto, and the Client Portal (V2 and Axi Select). The same components power onboarding flows that satisfy 180+ jurisdictions and the order ticket that handles leveraged Perpetual Futures.

Governance and process

Lokalise integration

Lokalise was a major leadership contribution noted in my Performance Review — I led the implementation across all designs to support different languages.

Outcomes

  • System spans 4 active products — ATP mobile, ATP web, ATP Crypto, Client Portal — with one design language and one component library
  • 4 designers + 4 developers on the system team, supporting 3 PMs and 4 engineering teams across UK, India, Philippines
  • 12 languages localised via Lokalise integration with the system
  • AA accessibility compliance maintained across colour and typography systems
