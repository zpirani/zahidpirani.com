Overview

Axi's product portfolio spans iOS, Android, Web, and a Client Portal — built by 4 engineering teams across 3 countries. Without a shared design language, teams were rebuilding components independently, creating inconsistencies that slowed delivery and eroded product quality.

I built, maintained, and evolved the Axi Design System to give every team a single source of truth for components, patterns, and interaction standards.

What the System Covers

  • Foundations — colour, typography, spacing, motion, and iconography tokens
  • Core components — buttons, inputs, tables, charts, modals, and navigation patterns
  • Trading-specific patterns — order tickets, position cards, price tickers, P&L indicators, and candlestick chart wrappers
  • Platform variants — mobile-specific and desktop-specific adaptations of shared components
  • Localisation — components designed to accommodate 12 languages including RTL layouts

Governance

A design system is only as good as its adoption. I established contribution guidelines that allowed product teams to propose new components, documented usage and anti-patterns for every component, and ran regular alignment sessions with engineering leads to ensure design and code stayed in sync.

Contribution workflow — structured proposal process for new components, with review, approval, and documentation gates

Outcome

The design system became the backbone of Axi's product development. New features that previously required weeks of design and engineering groundwork could be assembled from existing components in a fraction of the time. Cross-team consistency improved measurably — and when Axi Crypto and the ATP Mobile redesign launched, they were built almost entirely on system components.
