By 2023, retail crypto trading was the fastest-growing area of the broker market. Axi made the strategic call to launch two crypto products together: Spot (buy and hold) and Perpetual Futures (continuous-exposure derivatives with leverage). All crypto design work was assigned to me.

I led the design end-to-end across both products on both surfaces — mobile and web — from launch. I worked with 2 designers on ATP, 3 Product Managers, and 4 engineering teams across the UK, India, and the Philippines. Engagement metrics here were measured on the mobile cohort via Amplitude.

Context

ATP launched in 2021 as a multi-asset trading platform — Forex, Commodities, Indices, Shares. Crypto was the natural next expansion: by 2023, retail crypto trading was the fastest-growing area of the broker market, and competitors were aggressively adding offerings.

Axi made the strategic call to launch two crypto products together:

  • Spot — buy and hold cryptocurrencies, no leverage
  • Perpetual Futures — continuous-exposure derivatives with leverage, no expiry dates

Spot is straightforward for new users; Perp is sophisticated, high-margin, and built for active traders.

All crypto design work was assigned to me. I led the design end-to-end across both products on both surfaces (mobile and web) from launch.

The challenge

Three problems sat at the centre of every crypto design decision:

  1. Make leverage understandable without making it boring. Perpetual Futures with leverage is a sophisticated instrument. Most retail traders haven't used futures before, even if they've held crypto. The design had to make leverage, margin, and liquidation risk legible at the point of trade — not buried in education.
  2. Honour 24/7 markets in a platform designed for session-based assets. ATP was built around Forex and equities — markets that close. Crypto never closes. Patterns like "market opening soon," "after-hours pricing," and overnight margin behaviour didn't apply. The UX had to be reconsidered around continuous-exposure assumptions.
  3. Ship cross-platform from day one. Unlike ATP itself (mobile-first for two years before web), crypto had to launch on both surfaces simultaneously. By this point the Axi Design System was mature enough to support it — but crypto was the first product to genuinely use that capability from launch.

The two products

Crypto Perpetual Futures

The headline launch. Perp gives traders continuous exposure to crypto markets with leverage — no expiry dates, more flexibility than dated futures, more leverage than spot.

The design challenges:

  • Leverage selection — surfacing leverage clearly at the point of trade, with margin requirements and liquidation price visible alongside
  • Real-time margin display — as positions move, margin used and available change. The UX had to update without overwhelming
  • Liquidation risk visualisation — the most consequential outcome for a leveraged position, and the easiest one to design badly
  • Funding rate clarity — Perp has funding rate payments that shift the long/short balance. The UI had to make these legible without requiring a derivatives education

Crypto Spot

The accessibility ramp. Spot is straightforward — buy crypto, hold crypto, sell crypto. No leverage. Simpler UX, broader audience.

The design challenges:

  • Make the spot/perp distinction clear without making spot feel like the "training wheels" option
  • Reuse ATP's existing market-browsing and order-ticket patterns without forcing crypto into a Forex-shaped hole
  • Wallet-style affordances — spot users think in terms of holdings, not positions. The UI had to bridge that mental model with ATP's existing position-management language

Cross-platform parity from day one

Unlike the broader ATP product (mobile-first for two years before web), crypto launched on both surfaces simultaneously. By this point, the Axi Design System was mature enough to support cross-platform launches by default — but crypto was the first product to genuinely use that capability from day one.

Outcomes

Engagement metrics measured on the mobile cohort via Amplitude. Web was instrumented but mobile is the data I retain.

  • 200% month-on-month growth in active trades on Crypto Perpetual Futures since launch
  • Cross-platform parity from day one — both Perp and Spot shipped on mobile and web simultaneously
  • First crypto-native UX in ATP — patterns that later informed how the broader product approached 24/7 markets and leveraged products
